## Track 4 — Per-commit Doodlestein

**Strategic**: `warn_searched_at` is captured *after* the failed inner call rather than before it, so it over-estimates the actual search timestamp by however long `record_search_audit` took to fail — move the capture to just before the `record_search_audit` call, or better yet pass `searched_at` as a parameter from the call-site that already has the real timestamp.

**Adversarial**: `rank as i64` silently truncates on any platform where `usize > i64::MAX` — harmless in practice, but the cast is unchecked; use `i64::try_from(rank).unwrap_or(i64::MAX)` or assert at the top of the loop to be explicit.

**Regret**: The `best_effort` wrapper's decision to bump `METRIC_AUDIT_WRITE_FAILURES` via a separate `increment_metric` call that also acquires the same Mutex is the most likely friction point — a lock-contention bug or a second failure there goes unobserved, and once callers start hitting it under load this silent swallow pattern will likely be replaced with a channel-based or atomic counter approach.
