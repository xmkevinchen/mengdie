## Lead disposition

All three findings concur on a single underlying concern: that the BL-015 close-out approach (project-wide explicit `PRAGMA foreign_keys = OFF`) might be the wrong shape. They are rejected because they disagree with the project's settled posture, not because they are unreasonable.

- **Strategic: REJECT** — Discussion 029 YAGNI 1 explicitly states "PRAGMA foreign_keys stays OFF project-wide". The audit hooks themselves rely on FK-OFF for graceful handling of concurrent-delete races (a memory_entries row deleted between search and audit-write must not block the audit-write — best-effort wrapper contract). Scoping FK-OFF to migration replay only would re-introduce that race in the audit path. This finding inverts the decision.
- **Adversarial: REJECT** — Plan F-002 Step 4 explicitly mandates drop-table failure injection: "Drop-table is the right injection here: invalid fact_id INSERTs would NOT fail under PRAGMA foreign_keys = OFF." Seeding valid memory_entries rows for the drop-table tests would mask the strict-helper's Err contract — a `tx.execute` against a dropped table fails for the right reason; an FK constraint violation fails for the wrong reason. The test design depends on FK-OFF.
- **Regret: WAIVE (probability of reversal: low)** — Reversal of the global `PRAGMA foreign_keys = OFF` write would require a project-wide FK-enforcement decision (e.g. v1 might choose strict FK enforcement; if so, BL-013 orphan-cleanup must complete first). Such a reversal would be a deliberate plan, not a casual edit. Codex Track 2 of THIS review independently confirmed all three supersession-SQL tests are correct under the FK-OFF posture; Codex Track 2 of Step 1 review explicitly recommended the explicit `PRAGMA foreign_keys = OFF` write. The BL-015 approach has been validated by two independent Codex reviews.
